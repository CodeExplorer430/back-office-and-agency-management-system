1. What CT2 Is

CT2 stands for Core Transaction 2. In the Travel and Tours ERP, CT2 is the back-office operating system. It helps the company manage internal travel operations after a booking or transaction already exists. It is focused on coordination, approvals, resource planning, supplier follow-up, promotions, visa assistance, and reporting.

Simple explanation: CT2 is the part of the ERP that helps employees organize and control operations behind the scenes.

Speaking cue: “If CT1 captures and manages the actual customer booking transaction, CT2 manages the back-office work needed to support that transaction.”

2. Where CT2 Fits In The Whole ERP

The Travel and Tours ERP is not only one system. It is a collection of connected systems that support different business areas such as human resources, logistics, administration, finance, booking operations, and back-office work. CT2 belongs to the travel-and-tours operational side, but it is focused specifically on internal coordination.

ERP area Main purpose How CT2 relates to it
Core Transaction 1 Customer booking and main tour transaction processing CT2 uses booking, customer, or payment references from CT1 when back-office follow-up is needed
Financials General ledger, AP/AR, collection, budget, and official accounting CT2 produces operational reports and financial snapshots, but Financials remains the official accounting system
Logistics and Fleet Procurement, warehouse, fleet, dispatch, and transport optimization CT2 plans travel resources and dispatch for tours, while enterprise-wide fleet ownership may still belong to logistics systems
Human Resources Employee records, performance, scheduling, compensation, and development CT2 uses staff information for operational assignment and coordination, but HR remains the master system for employee lifecycle data

Panel-ready point: CT2 is a connected subsystem inside the ERP, not a standalone island. It focuses on internal operational workflow, while other ERP systems remain the source of truth for their own domains.

5. General Process Flow Of CT2

  1. A user logs into CT2.
  2. The dashboard shows pending back-office work.
  3. The user opens the correct module depending on the task.
  4. The user views data, updates workflow state, uploads documents, or triggers an approval.
  5. If a decision is sensitive, the request goes through the approvals process.
  6. The system records the action in the audit trail.
  7. The result appears in reports, dashboards, or exports for management use.

Speaking cue: “The easiest way to understand CT2 is to see it as the internal workflow engine of the travel operations team. It receives references from the main transaction flow, then organizes the support work needed to complete operations.”

6. Example End-To-End Scenario

Scenario: a customer booking needs back-office support

  1. A booking is created in Core Transaction 1.
  2. CT2 uses the booking reference for internal follow-up.
  3. The supplier module checks whether the required partner is approved and ready.
  4. The availability module assigns transport or resources for the trip.
  5. If there is a promotion, the marketing module tracks the related campaign or voucher.
  6. If the trip requires a visa, the visa module tracks checklist items and document uploads.
  7. The approvals module handles any pending decisions.
  8. The financial module summarizes the operational result for reporting.

Simple explanation: CT2 connects the support work around the booking, even if the booking itself started somewhere else.

7. How CT2 Connects To Other Systems

CT1 connection

CT1 is closer to customer booking transactions, itinerary scheduling, reservations, and customer-facing processing. CT2 can use CT1 references such as booking IDs, customer IDs, and payment references so the back-office team can do the supporting operational work.

Financials connection

CT2 can create financial reports, flags, and exports for operations, but it should not replace the Financials module for official accounting, general ledger, accounts payable, accounts receivable, and collections.

HR connection

CT2 may use staff data for assignments and operations, but HR systems remain responsible for the full employee lifecycle such as onboarding, timekeeping, payroll, and performance.

Logistics connection

CT2 can schedule tour resources, vehicles, drivers, and dispatch information for operational use. If the company uses a broader fleet or logistics system, CT2 should work beside it rather than replace it.

9. Why CT2 Is Important

  • It centralizes back-office operations in one controlled system.
  • It reduces scattered manual tracking across spreadsheets or separate tools.
  • It gives managers a structured approval and monitoring process.
  • It improves visibility over suppliers, resources, marketing actions, and visa support work.
  • It produces operational reports that help management decision-making.

10. Security And Reliability In Presentation Terms

Even though CT2 is a student project presentation subject, it is built with real operational controls. Users must log in, access is permission-based, sensitive writes are protected, and important actions are logged. The project also includes automated validation before release so changes are tested before deployment.

Panel-ready point: CT2 is not only a CRUD system. It includes workflow control, permission control, validation, and deployment checks, which makes it closer to a real internal enterprise subsystem.

13. Possible Questions From Technical Panelists

Possible question Suggested answer
What is the difference between CT1 and CT2? CT1 is closer to core booking and customer transaction processing, while CT2 is focused on back-office operational workflow and internal coordination.
Does CT2 store all data of the ERP? No. CT2 stores workflow-specific data and references other systems when needed. It is not the owner of all master records.
Why is CT2 modular? Because back-office work covers different domains such as suppliers, availability, marketing, financial summaries, and visa support. Each module handles a specific part of the workflow.
How does CT2 interact with other systems? It uses external IDs and business references, such as booking or payment references, and coordinates internal work based on those connected records.
Why not put everything in one system only? Because each subsystem has a different business responsibility. CT2 is specialized for back-office control, while other systems remain responsible for their own domains.
What makes CT2 secure? It uses login authentication, permission checks, protected form submissions, audit logging, and validation before deployment.

14. Final Presentation Summary

CT2 is a focused back-office subsystem inside a larger Travel and Tours ERP. Its role is to manage internal workflows that support the business after or around a transaction, such as supplier follow-up, resource planning, approvals, promotions, visa processing, and reporting. It is best understood as a workflow and coordination engine connected to other enterprise systems, not as the single owner of all enterprise data.

Final speaking cue: “Our system is important because it turns complex back-office travel operations into a structured, trackable, and secure workflow. That is the real role of CT2 in the ERP.”
